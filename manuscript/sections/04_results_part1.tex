\subsection{RQ1: attribution is tail-robust but architecture-dependent}\label{subsec:results_family}

On 5,832 common observations, mean $D$ is positive under the cash-flow anchor (0.00237--0.00243) but negative under the balance-sheet anchor ($-0.00618$ to $-0.00612$), despite about 80-percent directional agreement at the observation level. Across model families, any discordance falls from 21.4 to 10.5 percent as the tolerance rises from zero to one percent; hard opposite classifications fall from 16.9 to 6.4 percent (Appendix Table~\ref{tab:family_discordance}). Because residual scales differ, these raw magnitudes are descriptive within architecture rather than directly comparable across model families. The revised industry-fixed-effect, industry-specific-slope, trailing-five-year, and historical-scale-normalised comparisons are pending rerun. The adapted Dechow--Dichev contrast is close to zero for the version-consistent states, while mapped-reversal contrasts are positive. Firm-level calibration is also unstable: 25.3 percent of firms remain in the same residual-volatility quartile, 22.7 percent move by one quartile, and 19.4 percent move between the bottom and top quartiles. Detailed results appear in Appendix~\ref{app:dd_details}. These checks reinforce an architecture boundary rather than provide CFO-independent validation.
