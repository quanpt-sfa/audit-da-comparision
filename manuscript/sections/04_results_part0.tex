\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Analysis population and direct reporting-state differences}\label{subsec:results_sample}

The analysis begins with 8,584 issuer-years for which both constructed pre-audit and post-audit annual states are available. Of these, 8,461 have complete profit, CFO, total-accrual, and beginning-asset measures for the direct comparisons. The historically estimated pooled post-audit-reference accrual specifications yield 7,801 issuer-years for the primary RQ1 and RQ2 model analyses. Because the source panel begins in 2016, the first estimable test year is 2017; all reported model tests therefore use historical audited observations strictly preceding the test year.

Table~\ref{tab:descriptive_statistics} shows that direct reporting-state differences are concentrated in CFO rather than profit. The median absolute CFO difference is 0.00444 of beginning assets, compared with 0.00025 for profit, a ratio of approximately 18 to one. Model-based quantities remain right-skewed, but considerably less so after the historical one-percent winsorisation prescribed in Section~\ref{subsec:rq1_design}; the modified-Jones post-audit-reference median $|DA^{pre}|$ is 0.0596 and its mean is 0.0927.

Table~\ref{tab:direct_revisions} reports the prespecified threshold sensitivity. At the common one-percent cutoff, CFO is material in 38.5 percent of pairs and profit in 7.3 percent. Under the asymmetric classification used in the research design, 32.0 percent of issuer-years have material CFO differences while profit remains stable; 6.2 percent have non-stable profit while CFO is not material. \subsection{RQ1: CFO is the larger attributed component}\label{subsec:results_rq1}

Table~\ref{tab:jones_family_attribution} reports the pooled post-audit-reference results. Across all four model families, the median absolute-component contrast is approximately 0.0023 of beginning assets and every issuer-cluster bootstrap interval excludes zero. CFO is the larger absolute Shapley component in 67.7 percent of issuer-years, with lower confidence bounds near 66 percent. For the two confirmatory Jones-family specifications, mean $D$ is 0.0051 under Jones and 0.0049 under modified Jones. The corresponding one-percent trimmed means are 0.0043 and 0.0042. The average CFO allocation is positive, whereas the average profit allocation is negative, but the confirmatory conclusion is based on the paired median absolute-component contrast rather than these means.

The signed allocations qualify the absolute-dominance result. Under modified Jones, both components reduce absolute discretionary accruals in 40.8 percent of observations. CFO reduces absolute discretionary accruals while profit increases them in 20.6 percent; profit reduces them while CFO increases them in 23.1 percent; both increase absolute discretionary accruals in 15.5 percent. Thus, CFO dominance does not imply that both reporting-state movements point toward a common lower-abnormality state.

The result is stable to the prespecified architecture and reference-state checks. Table~\ref{tab:architecture_sensitivity} shows that the median contrast remains between 0.00223 and 0.00233 across pooled, ICB-industry fixed-effect, industry-specific-slope, and trailing-five-year architectures. The CFO-larger share ranges from 67.5 to 67.8 percent. Pooled pre-audit-reference medians are also nearly identical to the post-audit-reference values. Industry-specific slopes retain 7,666 observations because cells below the 40-observation historical minimum are excluded.

The highest pre-audit-$|DA|$ decile is not necessary for the conclusion. Excluding that decile leaves a Jones median contrast of 0.00222 (95-percent CI: 0.00186--0.00273) and a modified-Jones median of 0.00223 (95-percent CI: 0.00184--0.00278). The corresponding CFO-larger shares are 68.5 and 68.4 percent. Mean $D$ is negative in the first six pre-audit-$|DA|$ deciles and positive in the upper four, indicating that a positive full-sample mean is tail-concentrated even though component dominance is not.

Table~\ref{tab:component_time_shift} reports the contemporaneous-pairing diagnostics. Under the cyclic within-issuer benchmark, realised median contrasts exceed simulated medians by approximately 0.0001 for both Jones and modified Jones. The Monte Carlo probability is below 0.001. The stricter independently sampled within-issuer and same-year/industry peer benchmarks produce similar point differences but substantially wider simulation distributions; their Monte Carlo probabilities range from 0.14 to 0.20. Consequently, the primary component-attribution conclusion is robust, whereas the additional claim that contemporaneous pairing is uniquely informative is supported by the cyclic benchmark but not by both stricter donor designs.
