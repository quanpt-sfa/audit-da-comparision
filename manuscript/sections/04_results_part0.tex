We therefore use medians for the RQ1 component-dominance diagnostic, means for decomposition accounting, and one-percent trimmed means to assess tail sensitivity.

Table~\ref{tab:direct_revisions} shows that the median absolute CFO difference is 18 times the median profit difference. CFO exceeds one percent of beginning assets in 38.5 percent of pairs, versus 7.3 percent for profit. CFO alone is material in 34.8 percent of pairs, profit alone in 3.6 percent, both in 3.7 percent, and neither in 57.9 percent. Under the study's asymmetric consequence thresholds, 32.0 percent of pairs are CFO-dominant and 6.2 percent are profit-dominant. These direct differences motivate component attribution rather than establish its result. The revised protocol also reports neighbouring 0.25-, 0.50-, 1.00-, and 2.00-percent asset-scaled thresholds so that the descriptive contrast is not tied to a single materiality convention; those sensitivity counts are included in the rerun manifest in Table~\ref{tab:protocol_extension_outputs}.

\subsection{RQ1: CFO carries the primary-model component attribution}\label{subsec:results_rq1}

Table~\ref{tab:jones_family_attribution} reports nearly identical Jones and modified-Jones results. Mean $D$ is about 0.0087, while the one-percent trimmed mean is about 0.0050, roughly 43 percent lower. The mean comprises a positive CFO component of about 0.0104 and a negative profit component of about $-0.0017$. CFO is the larger absolute component in 69.1 percent of observations. The median $|\phi^{CFO}|-|\phi^{PAT}|$ is 0.00262--0.00268, and both issuer-cluster intervals exclude zero. Architecture-scale-normalised contrasts, CFO-dominant shares under the new pooled-architecture sensitivities, and signed Shapley quadrants are pending rerun in Table~\ref{tab:protocol_extension_outputs}; raw contribution magnitudes are not compared across model families until those normalised outputs are available.
