# Post-baseline transition and falsification protocol

The baseline direction gate has passed, but the next stage does not treat nonzero absolute-DA reduction as direct evidence that an auditor intervened. It separates five questions:

1. Are extreme observations generated by total-accrual construction, source switching, scaling, or a concentrated 2024 data problem?
2. How many apparent near-zero reductions conceal DA sign flips?
3. Does the observed corrective tilt exceed a conditionally mean-zero noise benchmark?
4. Did normal-accrual predictive calibration deteriorate in 2021-2022 or 2024?
5. Which firm-years receive opposing classifications from classic and performance-adjusted accrual models?

The Jensen directional null applies only when the placebo adjustment has conditional mean zero. The pipeline therefore reports three placebos: raw within-stratum permutation, centered within-stratum permutation, and symmetric-sign magnitude permutation. The latter two are the directional falsification tests.

Placebos are restricted to common-reference benchmarks because only there does signed DA movement equal the observed total-accrual adjustment exactly.
